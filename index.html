Cultural and Linguistic Influences on LLM Responses

Supervised by Xiyang Hu

Short description ‚ñ∏

Large language models (LLMs) are not culturally neutral. Trained on vast corpora spanning diverse cultures and languages, they often inherit implicit cultural norms and linguistic patterns. This project investigates how linguistic and cultural contexts systematically influence LLMs' response and their downstream applications in mental health scenarios.

Through controlled experiments with bilingual prompts, we examine variations in response patterns and evaluate how these upstream variations impact downstream tasks. This research aims to reveal how culturally embedded constructs within LLMs shape user outcomes and provide guidance for designing culturally aware and equitable AI systems.

AI-generated Stories for Emotional Support

Supervised by Yifan Yu, Wendao Xue

Short description ‚ñ∏

Stories are central to how people understand the world and themselves. In mental health, stories are known to support healing by encouraging emotional processing, perspective-taking, and reflection.

This project introduces a novel conversational healing mode, where personalized and empathetic stories are generated by AI to help users engage emotionally in a meaningful way. We investigate whether this story-based design is effective and what behavioral mechanisms underlie its benefits.
